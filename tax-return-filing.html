Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about tax return filing

What documents do I need for income tax filing?

You'll need PAN card, income statements (salary slips, business income details), bank statements, investment proofs, expense records, and previous year's tax returns. We provide a detailed checklist based on your specific situation.

What is the deadline for filing VAT returns?

VAT returns (Form 2000) are due on the 25th of each month for the previous month's transactions. For quarterly filers, returns are due on the 25th of the month following the quarter end.

Do I need to file TDS returns if I'm a small business?

Yes, if you make any payments subject to TDS (salary, rent, professional fees, contract payments, etc.), you are required to deduct tax and file monthly TDS returns, regardless of your business size.

What happens if I miss a filing deadline?

Late filing incurs penalties and interest on any tax due. Penalties are typically 5% per month for VAT and TDS, and up to 200% of tax for income tax. We help you avoid these with timely reminders and filing.

Can you file returns for previous years?

Yes, we can assist with filing belated returns for previous years. However, interest and penalties may apply depending on how late the filing is. We'll advise you on the best approach.

What is the difference between TDS and advance tax?

TDS is tax deducted at source by the payer, while advance tax is paid directly by the taxpayer in installments during the year. Both are methods of collecting tax before the final assessment.
