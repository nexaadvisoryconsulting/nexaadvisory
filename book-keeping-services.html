    Frequently Asked Questions

    Common questions about our bookkeeping services

    What is the difference between bookkeeping and accounting?

    Bookkeeping is the systematic recording of daily financial transactions, while accounting involves interpreting, analyzing, and reporting that data. Bookkeeping is the foundation upon which accounting is built.

    How often will you update my books?

    We offer daily, weekly, or monthly updates based on your business needs and transaction volume. Most small businesses opt for weekly updates, while larger businesses may need daily updates.

    Do you use accounting software?

    Yes, we work with popular accounting software including Tally, QuickBooks, SAP, Xero, and various cloud-based solutions. We can also work with your existing systems or recommend the best software for your needs.

    What documents do you need from me?

    We'll need access to your bank statements, invoices, receipts, and any other financial documents. We can work with physical documents or set up digital access to your accounts for seamless integration.

    Is my financial data secure?

    Absolutely. We use secure, encrypted systems to store and transmit your data. We also sign strict confidentiality agreements with all clients and follow industry best practices for data security.

    Can you handle bookkeeping for multiple businesses?

    Yes, we work with business owners who have multiple entities. We maintain separate, distinct records for each business and can provide consolidated reporting if needed.
